proof of Tukey’s lemma


Let S be a set and F a set of subsets of S such that F is of finite character. By Zorn’s lemma, it is enough to show that F is inductive. For that, it will be enough to show that if (Fi)i∈I is a family of elements of F which is totally ordered by inclusion, then the union U of the Fi is an element of F as well (since U is an upper bound on the family (Fi)). So, let K be a finite subset of U. Each element of U is in Fi for some i∈I. Since K is finite and the Fi are totally ordered by inclusion, there is some j∈I such that all elements of K are in Fj. That is, K⊂Fj. Since F is of finite character, we get K∈F, QED.
